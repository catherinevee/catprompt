Student ID Cards

Your campus ID card is your key to university life. Use it for dining, library access, building entry, making purchases, and more. Get your card, manage your account, and access all campus services.

Spring ID Card Services

Extended Hours: The ID Card Office will have extended hours during the first two weeks of the semester to accommodate new students. Walk-ins welcome!

Getting Your First ID Card

New students can get their campus ID card during orientation week or by visiting the ID Card Office. The process is quick and easy!

1
Gather Required Documents

Bring a government-issued photo ID (driver's license, passport, or state ID) and your student enrollment verification.

Required: Photo ID, Enrollment verification, Payment for card fee ($25 for new students)
2
Visit the ID Card Office

Come to the Student Union, Room 120 during business hours. Walk-ins are welcome, but appointments are recommended during busy periods.

Location: Student Union Building, Room 120
Hours: Mon-Fri 8AM-5PM, Sat 10AM-2PM
3
Complete the Application

Fill out the ID card application form with your personal information and verify your student details.

Info Needed: Name, Student ID, Address, Emergency contact, Photo preferences
4
Take Your Photo

Our staff will take your ID photo. Make sure to dress appropriately and follow photo guidelines for the best result.

Photo Tips: Face camera directly, neutral expression, no hats or sunglasses, solid background
5
Receive Your Card

Your card will be printed immediately and activated for all campus services. Test it before leaving to ensure everything works properly.

Activation: Dining access, building entry, library services, and campus purchases ready immediately
Lost or Damaged Cards

Lost Your Card?

Report it immediately! Call (555) 123-4570 or use our online system to deactivate your card and prevent unauthorized use.

Replacement Process

  1. Report the loss immediately - Call the ID Card Office or use the online reporting system
  2. Deactivate your card - This prevents anyone from using it for purchases or access
  3. Request a replacement - Schedule an appointment or visit during walk-in hours
  4. Pay the replacement fee - $15 for standard replacement, $25 for expedited
  5. Get your new card - Usually available same day or next business day
Campus Access & Security

What Your ID Card Unlocks

  • Residence Halls: Your assigned building and floor access
  • Academic Buildings: Classrooms, labs, and study spaces
  • Library: Main library and branch locations
  • Recreation Center: Fitness facilities and sports equipment
  • Dining Facilities: All dining halls and food courts
  • Computer Labs: Technology centers and study areas
  • Parking Garages: Student parking areas (with permit)
  • Special Events: Campus activities and restricted venues

Security Features

  • Encrypted Data: All information is securely encrypted
  • Access Logging: All card uses are tracked for security
  • Photo Verification: Your photo helps verify identity
  • Expiration Date: Cards automatically expire for security
  • Immediate Deactivation: Lost cards can be instantly disabled
  • Backup Systems: Multiple verification methods available

Security Reminders

Never lend your ID card to others. Don't share your PIN. Report suspicious activity immediately. Keep your card in a safe place when not in use.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take to get a new ID card?

Most ID cards are produced immediately while you wait. During busy periods (first weeks of semester), there may be a short wait of 15-30 minutes.

Can I use my ID card off-campus?

Some local businesses accept campus ID cards for payment. Check with individual merchants. Your card also works at other universities that participate in reciprocal programs.

What if my card stops working?

Cards can stop working due to damage, demagnetization, or account issues. Visit the ID Card Office for testing and replacement if needed.

Can I get a photo retake?

Photo retakes are allowed once per academic year for $10. Additional retakes are $15 each. Photos must meet university standards.

How do I add money to my card?

Add funds online through the student portal, at campus kiosks, in person at the ID Card Office, or set up auto-reload from a bank account or credit card.

What happens to my balance when I graduate?

Remaining balances can be refunded to you after graduation. Requests must be made within 30 days of graduation. Small balances under $5 may be donated to student emergency funds.
