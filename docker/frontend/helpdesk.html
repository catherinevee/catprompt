Frequently Asked Questions

You can reset your password using the self-service portal at password.university.edu. You'll need your student ID and access to your recovery email or phone number. If you can't access these, visit the IT Help Desk with a photo ID.

First, make sure you're connecting to "Campus-Secure" with your student credentials. Try forgetting the network and reconnecting. If issues persist, check our WiFi troubleshooting guide or contact support for device-specific help.

All students get free Microsoft Office 365. Visit office.com and sign in with your university email address. You can install Office on up to 5 devices and access online versions through any web browser.

Check our mobile email setup guide for step-by-step instructions. Make sure you're using the correct server settings and that two-factor authentication is properly configured. Contact us if you need help with the setup process.

Install the campus printing software from our downloads page. You'll need to be connected to Campus-Secure WiFi and have your student ID ready for authentication. Print jobs are charged to your student account.

We provide support for connecting personal devices to campus services (WiFi, email, etc.) and can help with university-provided software. For general device issues or personal software, we offer basic guidance but recommend contacting the manufacturer.
