WiFi Setup Guide

Connect your devices to the campus wireless network with these step-by-step instructions. Get secure, high-speed internet access throughout campus buildings and residence halls.

Network Status: All Systems Operational

Campus wireless networks are currently running normally. If you experience issues, try the troubleshooting steps below or contact the IT Help Desk.

Device-Specific Setup Instructions

Select your device type below for detailed, step-by-step setup instructions:

Windows Setup Instructions

1
Open Network Settings

Click the WiFi icon in the system tray (bottom-right corner) or go to Settings > Network & Internet > WiFi

2
Select Campus-Secure

From the list of available networks, click on "Campus-Secure". If you don't see it, click "Show available networks" to refresh the list

3
Enter Your Credentials

Username: Your student ID number
Password: Your student portal password
Click "Connect" when done

4
Accept Security Certificate

A security certificate dialog will appear. Click "Connect" or "Accept" to proceed with the secure connection

5
Verify Connection

You should now be connected. The WiFi icon will show as connected, and you can browse the internet

Mac Setup Instructions

1
Open WiFi Settings

Click the WiFi icon in the menu bar or go to System Preferences > Network > WiFi

2
Select Campus-Secure

From the dropdown list, select "Campus-Secure" network

3
Enter Credentials

Username: Your student ID number
Password: Your student portal password
Click "Join"

4
Trust Certificate

When prompted about the certificate, click "Continue" and then "Update Settings" to trust the network certificate

5
Confirm Connection

The WiFi icon will show full bars, and you'll have internet access throughout campus

iPhone/iPad Setup Instructions

1
Open WiFi Settings

Go to Settings > WiFi and make sure WiFi is turned on

2
Select Network

Tap "Campus-Secure" from the list of available networks

3
Enter Login Information

Username: Your student ID number
Password: Your student portal password
Tap "Join"

4
Trust Certificate

When the certificate warning appears, tap "Trust" to accept the campus security certificate

5
Connection Complete

You'll see a checkmark next to Campus-Secure and the WiFi icon in the status bar

Android Setup Instructions

1
Access WiFi Settings

Go to Settings > WiFi or swipe down and long-press the WiFi icon

2
Choose Network

Tap "Campus-Secure" from the available networks list

3
Configure Connection

EAP method: PEAP
Identity: Your student ID
Password: Your student password
Tap "Connect"

4
Accept Certificate

When prompted, tap "Connect" to accept the network certificate

5
Verify Connection

The network will show as "Connected" and you'll have internet access

Chromebook Setup Instructions

1
Open Network Settings

Click the status area (bottom-right) and select the WiFi icon, or go to Settings > WiFi

2
Select Campus Network

Click "Campus-Secure" from the list of available networks

3
Enter Network Details

EAP Method: PEAP
EAP Phase 2: MSCHAPv2
Identity: Your student ID
Password: Your student password

4
Connect to Network

Click "Connect" to join the network

5
Verify Internet Access

Once connected, you can browse the internet and access campus resources

Gaming Consoles & Smart Devices

Gaming consoles, smart TVs, and other devices that don't support enterprise WiFi can be registered for network access:

  1. Connect your device to "Campus-Device-Registration" network
  2. Open a web browser and go to register.university.edu
  3. Enter your student credentials and device MAC address
  4. Wait 10-15 minutes for registration to complete
  5. Connect to "Campus-Devices" network

Eduroam Network

As a visiting student or researcher, you can also use the international Eduroam network:

  • Network Name: eduroam
  • Username: yourid@university.edu
  • Password: Your student password
  • Available at thousands of institutions worldwide

Common Connection Issues

  • Can't see Campus-Secure: Make sure you're in a coverage area and refresh your WiFi list
  • Authentication failed: Verify your username is just your student ID (no @university.edu)
  • Certificate errors: Accept the campus certificate - it's safe and required for security
  • Slow speeds: Try disconnecting and reconnecting, or restart your device
  • Frequent disconnections: Update your device's WiFi drivers or contact IT support
Network Policies & Guidelines

Acceptable Use Policy

When using campus WiFi, please follow these guidelines:

  • Use bandwidth responsibly - avoid excessive streaming during peak hours
  • No illegal downloading or sharing of copyrighted content
  • Don't share your login credentials with others
  • Report security issues or suspicious activity immediately
  • Respect network resources and other users

Security Best Practices

  • Always connect to official campus networks (Campus-Secure, not open networks)
  • Keep your device operating system and antivirus software updated
  • Use HTTPS websites whenever possible for secure browsing
  • Don't access sensitive information on public computers
  • Log out of services when finished, especially on shared devices

Bandwidth Management

To ensure fair access for all users:

  • Peak hours (9 AM - 5 PM): Academic traffic prioritized
  • Evening hours (6 PM - 11 PM): General internet access
  • Late night (11 PM - 8 AM): No restrictions
  • Automatic throttling for excessive usage
